Research

The literature review provided insight into the well-documented dangers of traditional alerting mechanisms. The competitive analysis informed our understanding of how M*Modal fits into the ecosystem of medical software. Our field research shed light on physicians’ wants and needs, both expressed and inferred.

We had two goals for our research: understand physicians’ needs when entering patient data in Electronic Medical Records, and understand how a solution to these needs fits into the existing landscape of medi cal software.

Process Overview

Our research was divided into two halves: data gathering and data analysis. Data gathering included a literature review, field research, and a competitive analysis. Data analysis included modeling, synthesis, and visioning.

During the user research phase of our project, we conducted a literature review, a competitive analysis, and field research. The literature review involved a detailed review of scholarly articles about our problem space. For the competitive analysis, we researched M*Modal's competitive landscape. Finally, field research involved semi-structured interviews and contextual inquiries with medical professionals working in a variety of specialities and medical institutions across the United States.

Our data analysis allowed us to collect our findings into overarching themes and big ideas in our research. We analyzed our data from the ground up — we went from insights from individual interviews, to gathering these insights into a bigger picture, to using these insights to generate visions for the second half of our project.
